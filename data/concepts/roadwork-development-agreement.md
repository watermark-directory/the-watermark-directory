The **Roadwork Development Agreement** (RDA) is the contract under which the
data-center developer agrees to fund the public road work around the campus —
four roundabouts and two corridor rebuilds — while the County and Port Authority
take those roads into public maintenance in perpetuity. Allen County Resolution
#588-25 adopted it, effective 2025-09-15.

Its headline is a private gift: a "Company Contribution" of **$14,500,000**, set
against the Tetra Tech Opinion of Probable Cost of $14,223,081. Three clauses
complicate the word *private*, each read from the executed agreement:

- **§5.5** (the "Overpayment Amount") lets public grant awards — State 629 and
  ODOD Jobs & Commerce grants — backfill and refund the contribution to the
  Company; see the [[grant-refund clause]].
- **§9.13** requires the Authority to give the Company at least five business
  days' notice before releasing the agreement in response to a records request.
- **§9.17** exempts the Company from any competitive procurement for the work.

The RDA is one of the instruments examined in the records-withholding analysis
([[public-records-mandamus]]), where §9.13 sits alongside the NDA and CRA as a
developer-notice clause. What the clauses say is read from the document; the
characterization of the layers as a designed system is argument, labelled as such.
