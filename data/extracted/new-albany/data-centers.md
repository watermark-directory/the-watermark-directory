## The divide (critical geography)

The New Albany International Business Park is ~12,000 acres **straddling the Franklin–Licking county
line** (~9,000 ac inside New Albany city limits). `[verified]` The data-center mass is on the **Beech
Road corridor in LICKING County (39089)** — Jersey Twp / Pataskala / Etna — which drains **South Fork
Licking → Licking → Muskingum** (HUC 05040006). The city core (Franklin 39049) drains **Big Walnut
Creek → Scioto** (HUC 05060001). So the **site/surface-discharge side is Muskingum**, inverting the
onboarding profile's Scioto framing — `[verified]` for the addresses, `[inference]` for the basin
assignment (confirm with a flowline trace). **But the source water is Scioto/Columbus:** the cluster's
cooling draw is on the **City of Columbus** municipal system, so `basin="scioto"` holds for the
*source-water* screen even though *surface drainage* is Muskingum. Intel is the clearest case below.

## 1 — Intel "Ohio One" (anchor; advanced manufacturing, not a data center)

- **Operator:** Intel Corporation. `[verified]`
- **Location:** ~1,000-acre megasite, **Jersey Township, LICKING County**, Johnstown/New Albany. `[verified]`
- **Scale/investment:** raised $20B (2022) → **$28B (Mar 2024)**; up to 8 fabs; Mod 1 / Mod 2. `[verified]`
- **Power:** **100+ MW continuous, 8,760 hr/yr, "Six Sigma"**; AEP built a **~$95M, 500-MW "Green
  Chapel" substation** (idle through the delay). `[verified]`
- **Water + basin (the key wrinkle):** **~5 MGD** drinking water from the **City of Columbus** (Intel =
  Columbus' largest single user); 80–90% returned; **process wastewater → on-site treatment → Columbus
  SANITARY SEWER (Jackson Pike / Southerly → Scioto); NO direct surface discharge.** So Intel's water
  source AND effluent both tie to the **Scioto/Columbus** system though the site sits Licking-side. `[verified]`
- **Air PTI (Ohio EPA, issued Sept 20–21, 2022):** 4 fab cleanrooms, **28 boilers, 46 emergency
  generators, 1 fire pump, 6 silos, 125 cooling towers**, 4 N₂ vaporizers. `[verified]`
- **Stage:** under construction, **DELAYED — Mod 1 ops ~2030–31; Mod 2 ops ~2032.** `[verified]`

## Regulatory context `[reference]`

- **AEP Ohio data-center tariff — PUCO Case 24-508-EL-ATA:** order July 9, 2025, effective July 23,
  2025; new large DCs (≥25 MW) pay **≥85% of contracted capacity for up to 12 yr** (exit fee ~3 yrs'
  minimum charges); ends the Central-Ohio connection moratorium. New Albany/Columbus load is the
  documented driver. **OMA appeal to the Ohio Supreme Court (Nov 2025)**; the hyperscalers are
  challengers too. `[verified]`
- **Ohio EPA Draft Data-Center General NPDES Permit OHD000001** (statewide; cooling / blowdown /
  generator stormwater) — same instrument cited in the Springfield register; applies here. `[verified]`
- **Data-center sales-tax exemption (ORC §122.175)** statewide cost ~$1.6B/yr (2025); **DeWine paused
  new OTCA exemptions Aug 2025** pending a legislative study. Capture the pause instrument. `[verified]`

## Hydrology hook

- **Scioto / Big Walnut side:** **Big Walnut Creek 7Q10 = 35.43 cfs** (USGS 03229500, derived,
  `low-flow-7q10.derived.yaml`); supply gage 03228500. This is the receiving system for the city core
  AND the Columbus-WWTP path Intel's process water follows. `[verified]`
- **Muskingum / South Fork Licking side:** the **actual DC footprint** (Beech Rd corridor, Licking Co)
  drains South Fork Licking → Licking → Muskingum (HUC 05040006). **No derived/cited South Fork Licking
  7Q10 in corpus; the Scioto NPDES inventory does NOT cover this HUC.** `[open]`
- **Screen type:** abstraction/source-water, not effluent-vs-7Q10. The dominant signal is consumptive
  cooling draw on the **Columbus** water system: New Albany contracted **~16 MGD** of Columbus capacity
  (2015–16); **total tenant peak ~8 MGD (Apr 2026)**, plus **Intel ~5 MGD**. Cooling-draw inputs to pin
  from the air permits: Intel 125 cooling towers; Meta 1 GW direct-to-chip; QTS 222 MW. `[verified]`
