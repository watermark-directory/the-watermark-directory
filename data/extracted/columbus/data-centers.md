## Regulatory spine — AEP Ohio data-center tariff (PUCO 24-508-EL-ATA) `[verified]`/`[reference]`

The central "mature end of the complex mix" instrument for the Scioto — Columbus is AEP's HQ city and
the metro + New Albany load is the documented driver.

- **Docket:** PUCO **Case No. 24-508-EL-ATA**. Filed **5/14/2024**; stipulation **10/23/2024**;
  evidentiary hearings Dec 2024 + Jan 2025; PUCO **order 7/9/2025** (effective same day). `[verified]`
- **Applies to:** new/expanded data centers with peak load **≥ 25 MW** in AEP Ohio territory (plus
  mobile/crypto ≥ 1 MW); existing < 25 MW grandfathered. `[verified]`
- **Core terms:** **85% minimum demand (take-or-pay)** of subscribed capacity; **up to 12-yr** contract
  (4-yr ramp, ~8-yr commitment); **exit fee ~3 yrs' minimum charges**; collateral / financial-viability
  proof and load-study fees ($10K–$100K); ends AEP's Central-Ohio connection moratorium (in place since
  ~Mar 2023). `[verified]` (the per-year ramp schedule is `[reference]` — verify against the filed tariff.)
- **Signatories:** AEP Ohio, PUCO Staff, Ohio Consumers' Counsel, Ohio Energy Group, Ohio Partners for
  Affordable Energy, Walmart. A rival settlement was backed by the hyperscaler coalition (Amazon,
  Google, Microsoft, Meta). `[verified]`
- **Live status:** the **Ohio Manufacturers' Association appealed to the Ohio Supreme Court (Nov 2025)**;
  Amazon/Google/Meta/Microsoft are challengers too. `[verified]` OH Supreme Court case no. `[open]`.
- Same utility (#14006) and same **25 MW** trigger already cited in the Lima corpus (`docs/GRID.md`), now
  formalized — fold the docket into that citation.

## Hydrology hook (managed-supply abstraction screen)

- **Receiving / source water:** Scioto River **7Q10 = 515.73 cfs** (USGS 03234500, "at Higby," the
  mouth-ward proxy; `low-flow-7q10.derived.yaml`); Olentangy 13.53 (03226800), Big Walnut 35.43
  (03229500), Big Darby 11.24. Metro abstraction/supply pair = Scioto at Columbus (03227500) + Olentangy
  near Worthington (03226800). `[verified]`
- **Managed supply:** Columbus DPU draws from **three reservoirs — Griggs & O'Shaughnessy (Scioto) +
  Hoover (Big Walnut)** — pumping ~145 MGD average; building a 4th **$1.6B** plant (SW Delaware Co);
  raised rates +18% water / +8% sewer and is *"studying whether large water users like data centers are
  paying their fair share"*; initiated annexation of the ~2,800-ac Hoover Reservoir area (Aug 2025). `[verified]`
- **Screen type:** managed-supply abstraction (evaporative cooling draw vs. a reservoir-and-wellfield
  system), plus the conventional 7Q10 frame — the inverse of the Miami buried-valley sole-source case
  and distinct from Lima's effluent-vs-7Q10. Per-DC cooling draw `[open]` until the air PTIs / DC-NPDES
  give the heat-rejection figures.
