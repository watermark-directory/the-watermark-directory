# Testimony Deck — "Who Is the Customer?" (slide outline)

**Cory Parent · Select Committee on Data Centers · June 1, 2026** — the visual companion
to the [written testimony](bosc-written-testimony-2026-06-01.md).
**Source:** [bosc-testimony-deck-2026-06-01.pdf](../../documents/legal/bosc-testimony-deck-2026-06-01.pdf) (13 slides)

1. **Title** — Select Committee on Data Centers · "Who Is the Customer? Beneficial-ownership disclosure in Ohio data center development."
2. **One argument, made plainly** — Ohio extends public benefits without requiring developers to disclose who they are. Not opposed; bills (HB 706, HB 646, forecast bill) get a lot right; one question unanswered.
3. **The Gap** — Ohio subsidizes developers it cannot name (shell company holds the deed, signs the agreement, takes the abatement).
4. **The Definition** — only R.C. 122.175 definition; adopt one capacity-based, ownership-aggregating standard.
5. **Disclosure is the foundation, not a footnote** — cost causation, anti-speculation, PJM identity-sharing, the 25-MW threshold all depend on it.
6. **The Economics Ownership Hides** — commercial vs government cloud; the 20–30% GovCloud premium; FedRAMP / DoD impact levels.
7. **The Economics Ownership Hides — continued** — IL5/IL6 enclaves are structurally closed to local enterprise; enclave vs cluster.
8. **What extraction looks like — and what reciprocity looks like** — Ohio ($5.1B → 356 jobs / ~$281.9M loss) vs Virginia/Loudoun (~38% of general fund); community-benefits agreements.
9. **A technical caution on the sales-tax-exemption forecast** — 3–5 yr refresh cycles + AI-class rack costs understate the realized exemption.
10. **The bills already in motion get a great deal right** — HB 706 (cost causation), HB 646 (study), EFIA (speculation).
11. **A warning on HB 646** — a study commission without independent facility-level data is vulnerable to capture.
12. **Recommendations** — the six asks (beneficial-ownership disclosure first).
13. **Close** — "Ohio can welcome this industry —" and complete the framework by requiring disclosure.
