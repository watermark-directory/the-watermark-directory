## 1. Tile-drained Ohio cropland — what it exports (the baseline burden)

### Hanrahan, King, Duncan, **Shedekar** (2021) — cover crops & N/P loss, 40 Ohio fields
*J. Environ. Manag. 293:112910 · [doi](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jenvman.2021.112910) · source: Europe PMC + PubMed `[abstract]`*

- **40 fields**, northcentral Ohio; tile drainage + surface runoff; cover-crop (CC) vs none (NoCC).
- Tile **NO₃-N and total-N loads ~50% lower** under cover crops; monthly reduction **~1.0–2.6 kg N ha⁻¹** Jan–Jun.
- **Phosphorus moved the opposite way:** cover crops *increased* monthly tile **DRP by 0.4–12.1 g ha⁻¹** and **TP by 1.2–31.6 g ha⁻¹**; no significant net difference in tile discharge/DRP/TP.
- **Corpus use:** establishes that the dominant tile-export problem here is **nitrate**, and that the cheap N-control practice (cover crops) **does not fix dissolved P** — which is the Lake Erie / Maumee HAB driver. Sets why a *structural* retention/treatment project (OSU-Lima) is needed for P at all.

### Osterholz, **Shedekar**, Simpson, King (2024) — "new" P losses via tile drainage
*J. Environ. Qual. 53(2):241 · [doi](https://doi.org/10.1002/jeq2.20549) · source: CrossRef `[full-abstract+]`*

- **155 P applications** across Ohio & Indiana; weighted-regression (WRTDS) on tile DRP/TP.
- "New" (recently-applied) **P loss averaged 79.3 g DRP ha⁻¹ and 96.1 g TP ha⁻¹ — <3% of P applied**; the 8 largest exceeded **330 g DRP** or **575 g TP ha⁻¹**.
- New P = **14% of annual DRP, 5% of annual TP** from tile drains (legacy P dominates the rest).
- **Worst actors:** broadcast liquid manure > inorganic; surface broadcast > injected/incorporated; **late-fall applications** risk the largest losses.
- **Corpus use:** most tile-drain P is **legacy**, not this-season fertilizer — so the basin's P burden is a *standing stock* problem, reinforcing HYDROLOGY's "burden already maxed."

## 2. Drainage water management (DWM) — how much it actually reduces nitrate

### **Shedekar**, King, Fausey, Islam, Soboyejo, Kalcic, Brown (2021) — DWM via three evaluation approaches
*Agric. Water Manag. 243:106501 · [doi](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agwat.2020.106501) · source: Semantic Scholar `[full-abstract+]`*

- **One central-Ohio field, 10 years** (free drainage 2005–2008; DWM 2009–2014); before-after, paired-field, and DRAINMOD-NII scenarios.
- DWM **reduced NO₃-N loads 9–25% (~2.8–10.3 kg ha⁻¹ yr⁻¹)** and **drainage discharge 8–23% (~19–69 mm)** —
- but **increased surface runoff ~50% (~13 mm)**, with little change in NO₃-N concentration (−1 to +2.5%).
- **Effectiveness varies widely by evaluation method** — the headline caution.

### King, Hanrahan, Stinner, **Shedekar** (2022) — field-scale DWM, paired NW-Ohio fields
*Agric. Water Manag. 264:107421 · [doi](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agwat.2021.107421) · source: RePEc `[qual]`*

- Paired NW-Ohio fields, **4 years**, before-after. Published abstract is **qualitative** (no kg/ha magnitudes reachable).
- Direction: **significant tile NO₃-N reduction at both sites**; **surface runoff up**, and **surface DRP/TP up** (significant at the higher-runoff site) — DWM's tile-P benefit can be **partly negated by surface-runoff P**.
- **Corpus use:** DWM is a real nitrate tool but **trades tile loss for surface runoff** — a caution against treating any single BMP as a clean win; the OSU-Lima design (capture + wetland + reuse) is precisely an attempt to close that surface-runoff path.

### Youssef … **Shedekar** … (2023) — controlled drainage & corn yield, regional synthesis
*Agric. Water Manag. 275:107993 · [doi](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.agwat.2022.107993) · source: SSRN preprint (1st-pass) `[abstract]`*

- **55 site-years, 13 paired free-/controlled-drainage sites** (6 Midwest states + NC, 2006–2017).
- **No significant average corn-yield difference** (CD 10.62 vs FD 10.53 Mg ha⁻¹); CD **raises yield in dry seasons, lowers it in wet** → argues for *actively-managed* CD.

## 3. Engineered P-removal structures — the OSU-Lima BMP family, and its limits

Directly relevant: the OSU-Lima grant builds **retention/treatment wetlands + a forebay**
to strip nutrients from ag runoff. Shedekar's two 2020 *Water* papers are the
**edge-of-field P-removal-structure** literature from the same team — and they are
notably **cautionary** about how well slag-based P capture works on *tile* water.

### **Shedekar**, Penn, Pease, King, Kalcic, Livingston (2020) — ditch structure, Al-treated steel slag
*Water 12(8):2149 (CC-BY) · [doi](https://doi.org/10.3390/w12082149) · source: Semantic Scholar `[abstract]` + MDPI econ snippet*

- Treats tile drainage from a **4.5 ha** high-soil-test-P Ohio field; media = **Al-treated steel slag**.
- Removed **~27% of dissolved P and ~50% of total P** → **2.4 kg DP, 9.4 kg TP** captured; removal **degraded / went variable** over time.
- As-built slag was **coarser / less sorptive** than the design samples → underperformance.
- **Economics:** structure cost **up to ~$10,000**; **~$100–1,500 per kg P removed** (snippet of MDPI text).

### Penn, Livingston, **Shedekar**, King, Williams (2020) — field-scale steel-slag structures
*Water 12(2):443 (CC-BY) · [doi](https://doi.org/10.3390/w12020443) · source: Semantic Scholar `[abstract]`*

- **~97% of P arrives during storm-flow events**; structures sized for **~12 L s⁻¹**.
- **Subsurface structure removed 19.2 kg (55%)** of cumulative dissolved-P; **ditch structure 0.9 kg (37%)** — both **below lab expectations**, with **flow inhibition over time** and a collapse in DP removal once pH < 8.5.
- **Recommendation: do not use non-treated steel slag for tile-drainage DP removal unless the media is replaced every 4–6 months** (bicarbonate in tile water precipitates CaCO₃, clogging pores).

## Synthesis for the corpus

1. **The basin's nutrient burden is real and quantified.** Tile-drained Ohio cropland
   exports nitrate at **kg/ha** scale and dissolved P at **g/ha** scale, with P dominated
   by **legacy stock** (Osterholz: new P <3% of applied, ≤14% of annual DRP). This is the
   measured backdrop for HYDROLOGY's "burden already maxed."
2. **The cheap N practice doesn't touch P.** Cover crops cut tile N ~50% but **raise**
   tile DRP/TP (Hanrahan). So P abatement requires **structural** interventions — exactly
   the OSU-Lima retention/wetland project's premise.
3. **Even structural P capture under-delivers on tile water.** The same team's P-removal
   structures hit only **37–55% DP removal**, degrade over months, and cost **$100–1,500/kg
   P** — a sober prior for what a **$650k** GLRI/Ohio-EPA grant ([capture](osu-lima-ag-runoff-treatment.capture.yaml))
   buys, and a reason its design leans on **wetland + reuse/irrigation** rather than slag.
4. **No BMP is a clean win.** DWM cuts tile nitrate **9–25%** but **shifts water to surface
   runoff (+50%)** and can raise surface P (Shedekar 2021; King 2022). Any claim that a new
   basin load is "offset" by conservation practice should be read against these ceilings.
