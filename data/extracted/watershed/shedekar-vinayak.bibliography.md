17. **2021** · *Cover crops differentially influenced nitrogen and phosphorus loss in tile drainage and surface runoff from agricultural fields in Ohio.* Hanrahan, King, Duncan, **Shedekar** (senior). **J. Manag.** 293:112910. **[full text]** — Continuous edge-of-field monitoring across **40 Ohio fields**: cover crops cut tile **nitrate-N and total-N loads ~50%** in the critical winter/spring window, but **phosphorus behaved oppositely and variably** (tile dissolved-P sometimes *increased* while surface-runoff P fell), with no significant net P difference. ## 5. Themes

Four threads run through the work: (1) **Drainage water management / controlled
drainage** — field + multi-site synthesis on how managing tile outflow affects
nitrate loss, water budgets, and crop yield (2012, 2015, 2020–2023). (2)
**Edge-of-field nutrient (N & P) loss & conservation practices** — cover crops,
gypsum amendments, and engineered steel-slag/woodchip P-removal structures, monitored
across large Ohio field networks (2020–2025). (3) **Hydrologic & water-quality
modeling** — DRAINMOD, RZWQM2-P, SWAT, multi-scale watershed models, validated against
edge-of-field data in the Western Lake Erie Basin. (4) **Monitoring methods** — the
tipping-bucket rain-gauge calibration line (2009 → 2016). Author position tracks a
clear arc from mid-list contributor (2012–2017) to **lead / senior author and PI**
(2020 onward).
