# Ohio Department of Development (ODD) — reference documents

Source documents from the Ohio Department of Development providing statewide economic
development context. Both are public-record publications, not litigation evidence.

## Documents

### `11062023-PIA-Decade.pdf`

**Priority Investment Areas map**, October 2023. Published under ORC 122.16.
One-page map showing Ohio counties classified as Distressed or Labor Surplus, and cities
classified as Distressed Cities, Labor Surplus Cities, or Inner City Distress.

**Allen County (Lima):** designated **Labor Surplus County**. Valid until reassessment
following the 2030 U.S. Census.

Distressed Counties are the higher-priority tier; Labor Surplus is one rung below but
still unlocks priority access to most ODD programs.

### `Final_PY25-29_Con_Plan.pdf`

**Program Year 2025–2029 Ohio Consolidated Plan**, June 2025. Prepared by the Ohio
Department of Development Community Services Division (CSD). Required by HUD; covers
the CDBG, HOME, ESG, HOPWA, and NHTF programs administered by ODD.

Relevant to Allen County:

- Allen County is a **non-entitlement county** — CDBG flows through the state, not
  directly to the county.
- Allen County is in **BoSCoC Region 12** (Balance of State Continuum of Care, with
  Auglaize and Mercer counties) — not an Entitlement County.
- Population change 2010–2020: **−3.9%** (mid-tier decline).
- Median household income: **88% of state MHI** (~$59K vs. Ohio's $67K, 2018–2022 ACS).

Contains the Needs Assessment (NA-05 and following sections), the population change
and MHI maps, and the five-year strategic plan and goals.
