## How she appears in the record

Cynthia "Cindy" Leis occupies **two seats at once** in the Project BOSC corpus:

- **President/CEO of the Allen Economic Development Group (AEDG)** — she signs the
  Elida Local School District notice letters as AEDG President/CEO (`leisc@aedg.org`),
  and the PAAC board minutes track her rise from AEDG Business Development Director
  to President/CEO by August 2024.
- **Executive Director of the Port Authority of Allen County (PAAC)** — she signs the
  PAAC records policy in that capacity, and recurs throughout the board minutes
  reporting on the deal (e.g. 2026-03-30, tying the N. Cole Street roadway
  improvements to "the Google data center project").

## Why the dual role matters

PAAC has **no employees** of its own; it must be administered by an external entity
([records policy](../extracted/aedg/paac-records-policy.policy.yaml)). With Leis serving as
**both** AEDG President/CEO and PAAC Executive Director, the same individual sits on
both sides of the front-end developer-information shield (R.C. 4582.58 — developer
information routed *through AEDG* is treated as a non-record until the employer commits
in writing) **and** the records custody for the public authority that approves the
deal. That single locus of control over the econ-dev shield + records is the core of
the records-completeness concern. See the memory note `leis-aedg-paac-dual-role`.

In the board minutes she describes roundabout phasing to objecting residents
(2025-08-07), and the PAAC board invokes the R.C. 4582.58(B) trade-secret shield to
enter executive session (2026-03-30) — the shield in active use.
