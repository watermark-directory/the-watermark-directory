## How he appears in the record

Russ Decker is the **Nutrien** representative at the Allen County Commissioners'
**Tax Abatement Informational Meeting on 2024-12-19**. The primary commissioners
minutes ([A121924.pdf](../documents/commissioners/meetings/A121924.pdf)) record
the agenda item — *"2:00 p.m. Tax Abatement Informational Meeting – Cindy Leis,
AEDG"* — and the Periplus minutes-flag-report OCR captures the narrative:

> "Tax Abatement Informational Meeting – Cindy Leis, AEDG and **Russ Decker,
> Nutrien**. Cindy Leis provided an overview of a new project at Nutrien that they
> have applied for a **50% property tax abatement for 10 years** – Shawnee School
> District and Apollo has been notified and Shawnee Township were presented the
> project on December 9th and they approved the tax abatement."

He also "provided an overview of the [project]" and the minutes note **Enterprise
Zone Agreement revisions**, with **Brad Niemeyer** to prepare a resolution and send
it to **Brittany Woods** to place on the agenda for approval.

## The tie-in (why he's tracked here)

Decker is not a BOSC actor — he matters because he shows the **AEDG playbook is a
pattern, not a one-off for the data center**. The Nutrien abatement runs through the
exact same machinery as the Project BOSC CRA:

- **[Cynthia Leis](cynthia-leis.md)** (AEDG) brokers and presents the abatement — the
  same dual-hatted AEDG/PAAC official at the center of BOSC.
- **[Brad Niemeyer](brad-niemeyer.md)** prepares the implementing resolution — the
  same County official who is the BOSC Roadwork Development Agreement contact.
- **[Brittany Woods](brittany-woods.md)** (Clerk of the Board) handles the agenda —
  the same clerk on the BOSC CRA.
- **School-district + career-center notice** (Shawnee School District + Apollo) mirrors
  the Elida-LSD school-notice step in the BOSC CRA
  ([school-district-notice-letters](../extracted/legal/prr-mandamus/school-district-notice-letters.notice.yaml)).
- The vehicle is an **Enterprise Zone / abatement**, the AEDG product the County
  renews with AEDG every year by resolution (e.g. Res #45-24, #62-25).
