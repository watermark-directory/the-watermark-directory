# Economic baseline reference (Allen County, OH — FIPS 39003)

Committed localized economic baseline for the corridor: employment by industry and
each sector's export-orientation, the quantitative ground beneath the data-center
deal. Regenerated by `bosc economics`; the site reads `baseline.yaml` (no live pull).

## What the numbers are

- **Total covered employment** — all-ownership county total (QCEW `agglvl 70`,
  `own 0`).
- **Sector employment** — private employment by 2-digit NAICS sector (QCEW
  `agglvl 74`, `own 5`), ranked by jobs.
- **Location quotient (`lq_annual_avg_emplvl`)** — the county's employment share in a
  sector divided by the national share. **> 1** means the county does more of that
  work than the nation (export-oriented); **< 1** means under-represented. QCEW
  publishes this directly; it is the standard county-level proxy for an
  **import/export ratio**.

## Known gaps & caveats

- **Population** is the US Census ACS 5-year estimate (`B01003_001E`), pulled for
  2010/2015/2020/2023. The Census API requires a key (`WATERMARK_CENSUS_API_KEY`); without
  one, employment + location quotients still regenerate and `baseline.population` is
  left null (the site page says so) rather than committing unverified figures. ACS
  5-year is a rolling estimate, not a point-in-time count.
- **No clean county import/export series exists.** Census USA Trade and the FT900 are
  state/port-district level, not county. The location quotient is the honest proxy
  surfaced here — no trade ratio is fabricated.
- QCEW annual averages lag ~1 year (the latest annual is the prior calendar year).
  Figures are connector-pulled and regenerable; columns are selected by **name**, never
  by index.
