# FERC regulatory seam reference — FERC eLibrary + FERC Online (Form 1)

Committed FERC<->PUCO jurisdictional seam for the campus: which government sets the
rules for the campus's power. The Federal Power Act splits authority at the
wholesale/retail line — **FERC** governs wholesale sales, interstate transmission, and
the RTO (PJM) market rules; **PUCO** governs Ohio retail electric service, distribution,
and retail rates. This is the regulatory layer between the serving utility (#94) and the
federal energy-policy layer (#98) of the grid stack (epic #93, issue **#97**). The

## The jurisdictional map

`bosc.grid.ferc.derive_ferc_seam` encodes the wholesale/retail boundary and where the
campus falls:

- **FERC** (Federal Power Act sections 205/206) — wholesale sales of electricity,
  interstate transmission, and RTO/ISO market rules (PJM), **including** co-location /
  behind-the-meter arrangements at FERC-jurisdictional generators.
- **PUCO** (FPA section 201(b) reserves retail to the states) — Ohio retail electric
  service, local distribution, and retail rates / tariffs (intrastate).
- **Campus arrangement** — classified **PUCO retail (grid-served)**: the campus is a
  retail tariff customer of AEP Ohio (cross-referencing the #94 serving-utility
  identification), so its service and rate fall under PUCO retail jurisdiction. A
  behind-the-meter co-location at a FERC-jurisdictional generator would instead implicate
  FERC — the live policy question the captured dockets address.

This determines **which regulator sets the campus's price** — PUCO retail (grid-served)
vs FERC wholesale (behind-the-meter co-location) — the regulatory backdrop the
consumer-cost thread (#91) sits under.

## Known gaps & caveats

- **Dockets are transcribed from FERC eLibrary and `verify`-flagged.** Each carries its
  cited docket number (`ER24-2172`, `AD24-11-000`); confirm the number, disposition, and
  date against FERC eLibrary before relying on it. **No docket number is fabricated** —
  these are real FERC proceedings. The broad PJM policy entry is deliberately held at
  `confidence: low` and **not** pinned to a precise number (prefer omission over
  invention — epic #93 data discipline).
- **FERC Form 1 is captured as a cited pointer, not a transcribed figure.** No rate-base
  or operating-revenue number is asserted unless it can be confidently cited from the
  filing; the `rate_base` / `operating_revenue` fields are `null` until a value is
  transcribed (then `source: reference`, medium confidence, `verify`-flagged). Obtain the
  current vintage from FERC Online for Ohio Power Company.
- **The campus arrangement is a classification, not a determination.** PUCO retail
  (grid-served) is the most likely arrangement given the #94 AEP Ohio identification;
  verify against the campus's actual service agreement / tariff filing. A behind-the-meter
  co-location would move the seam to FERC.
- **General law vs specific facts.** The FERC<->PUCO jurisdictional split (the Federal
  Power Act sections) is cited at **high** confidence; the campus classification, the
  dockets, and the Form-1 pointer are at **medium** (or **low**) confidence and flagged
  for verification.
