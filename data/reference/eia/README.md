# Consumer energy-cost reference (Ohio) — US EIA

Committed consumer energy-cost baseline for the corridor: what households pay for
electricity and heating fuel, and the scale of the data-center load against the state
market. The consumer-price half of the demand thread (issue #91, from the 2026-06-10
facility-design call: *"bring in fuel costs at the consumer level due to macro
pressures and data-center demand"*). Regenerated by `bosc eia`; the site/scenario read
`consumer-energy.yaml` (no live pull).

## The demand → consumer-price-pressure sensitivity

`bosc.economics.energy.derive_demand_pressure` links the facility's first-class total
**`facility_draw`** (`bosc.facility.power`, issue #87 — IT load × PUE) to this dataset:

- **Annual consumption** = facility draw × 8760 h × load factor (~0.9, assumption).
- **Demand share** = that consumption ÷ Ohio total retail electricity sales (EIA).
- **Households-equivalent** = consumption ÷ ~10,500 kWh/household/yr (EIA US avg).
- **Price-pressure band** = demand share × a stylized demand-to-price transmission
  coefficient (0.5–1.0 %price/%demand).

The demand share and households-equivalent are the **robust, EIA-cited headline**. The
price-pressure band is a **stylized screening sensitivity, not a forecast** — retail
price formation is far more complex than one coefficient.

## Known gaps & caveats

- **EIA requires a key** (`BOSC_EIA_API_KEY`, free). Without one, `bosc eia` replays the
  committed offline fixtures (`tests/fixtures/economics/eia/`); a live keyed pull
  refreshes to the latest annual vintage. Figures here are EIA **annual averages**
  (latest stable annual; EIA annual data lags ~1 year).
- **The campus buys at wholesale/industrial rates, not the residential price.** The
  residential price is the *consumer-impact reference*, not the campus's own bill.
- **Retail fuel is state-level natural gas, not gasoline.** EIA retail gasoline is
  PADD/region-level (Midwest PADD 2), not state; we use Ohio residential natural gas
  ($/Mcf) as the state-level consumer heating-fuel series and do not fabricate a
  county/state gasoline figure.
- Series values are **connector-pulled and regenerable**; EIA columns are selected by
  **name** (`period` / `value`), never by index.

### `grid-profile.yaml` specifics (issue #94)

- **Serving utility (AEP Ohio) is corpus-grounded, not asserted.** The relator
  [data appendix](../../extracted/legal/select-committee-2026/relator-testimony/bosc-data-appendix-2026-06-01.md)
  references the **AEP Ohio tariff** for this campus's 25 MW threshold; the Allen County
  commissioners' minutes reference local AEP service (Res #974-25). The retail
  service-territory boundary is **formally confirmed** against the EIA-861 service-
  territory file / PUCO map — named as the confirmation source (`confidence: high`).
- **RTO = PJM** is authoritative (AEP Ohio is a PJM transmission zone), FERC-jurisdictional.
- **The AEP-Ohio (EIA-861) and PJM (EIA-930) load figures are transcribed published
  values** (`source: reference`, `confidence: medium`), flagged *verify* — the per-utility
  EIA-861 entity file and EIA-930 annual aggregation are the authoritative sources to
  regenerate against with a key/bulk pull. The **state** retail denominator is the
  connector-sourced figure shared with `consumer-energy.yaml` (#91).
- The campus load share derives from the first-class `facility_draw`
  (`bosc.facility.power`, issue #87 — IT load × PUE), at the same ~0.9 load factor as #91.

### `ba-interchange.yaml` specifics (issue #95)

- **EIA-930 region-data is hourly**; the connector **reduces it to window-mean
  aggregates inside the fetch** (demand mean/peak, net-generation mean, net-interchange
  mean/min/max, net-import-hours fraction) so the cached payload and committed fixture
  stay tiny. The window is a **representative summer month** (peak cooling demand,
  data-center relevant), not the full year — recorded in `period_start`/`period_end`.
- **Sign convention:** total interchange `TI` is **+ for net exports, − for net
  imports** (EIA-930). PJM is a net exporter on average.
- Keyed (`BOSC_EIA_API_KEY`); offline replays `tests/fixtures/economics/eia930/`. The
  committed values are real EIA-930 PJM magnitudes for the window, regenerable with a key.
- The derived `CampusInterchangeComparison` is a **screening** view over window means,
  **not** an hourly dispatch or locational (LMP/congestion) model — deliverability to the
  campus bus is the PJM-queue layer (#96).
