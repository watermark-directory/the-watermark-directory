Contributing to Watermark

Help build the record.

Watermark is built in the open, by people who want the record to exist. Three ways in: keep it funded, keep it running, or bring what you know.

Donations

Keep the record open.

Watermark is free to read, built to stay that way. Hosting, FOIA requests, and field research cost real money. Recurring support keeps the record funded and independent — no paywalls, no sponsored content, no data sales.

Code

The platform is open source.

Every line of Watermark — the record parser, the evidence viewer, the source-linking engine — is public and improvable. Bugs, gaps, and open feature work are tracked in the repo. No contributor agreement required for small fixes.

Research

Know something we don't?

The record grows from people closer to the ground. Leads come in as tips, document finds, or first-hand knowledge. We treat every submission as a lead, not a verdict — it goes into the open case file, labeled as such, until corroborated.

Submit a tip open
Something you noticed, heard, or saw near a site. A truck count, a utility pull, a conversation. No documentation required to start.
Submit →
Share a document verified
A permit, deed, filing, or record you found through a request or a public counter. Upload it directly — we'll process, cite, and publish it with full provenance.
Upload →
Request a correction scope gap
Something in the record that's wrong, outdated, or missing a source. Point us to the discrepancy — corrections are published with a provenance note.
Flag →
How we handle submissions

Every submission enters the open case file, labeled as a lead until a second source corroborates it. Sources are never named without consent. Submissions may be published in whole or in part, with provenance noted. We do not share contact information.
