# Grid & regulatory stack — utility → interchange → RTO → FERC → federal policy

The layer **above** the facility power model ([COMPUTE.md](COMPUTE.md) /
`bosc.facility.power`): the chain from the campus load up through the serving electric
utility, balancing-authority interchange, the PJM wholesale market, FERC's federal
jurisdiction, and federal energy policy. It is the macro backdrop the consumer-fuel-cost
thread ([ECONOMICS.md §6](ECONOMICS.md)) and the generation-cycle model ([COMPUTE.md](COMPUTE.md))
plug into. Epic **#93**; this page tracks the stack as its sub-issues land.

Like the other computed subsystems, every figure is provenance-tagged — numeric values
as `ProvenancedValue`, string identifications as `CitedFact` (the non-numeric analogue),
so a serving utility is **cited, never asserted**. External pulls follow the connector
contract (`fn(..., settings) -> pydantic` via `_cache.cached_get`, committed fixtures,
offline fallback) and land as regenerable committed datasets under `data/reference/`.

## #94 — serving electric utility & balancing-authority profile

The foundation layer (`bosc.grid.utility.derive_grid_profile` →
[`data/reference/eia/grid-profile.yaml`](../data/reference/eia/), regenerated by
`bosc grid`).

**The cited service chain.** The campus's retail electric utility is **AEP Ohio (Ohio
Power Company)** `[verified: document]` — the relator
[data appendix](../data/extracted/legal/select-committee-2026/relator-testimony/bosc-data-appendix-2026-06-01.md)
references the **AEP Ohio tariff** for this campus's 25 MW threshold, corroborated by
the Allen County commissioners' minutes (local AEP service, Res #974-25); the EIA-861
service-territory file / PUCO map is the formal confirmation source. The balancing
authority / RTO is **PJM Interconnection** (authoritative — AEP Ohio is a PJM
transmission zone, FERC-jurisdictional); retail service is **PUCO**-regulated.

**Campus load as a share of the grid.** The first-class `facility_draw`
(`bosc.facility.power`, [#87](COMPUTE.md) — IT load × PUE, ~348 MW central) at a ~0.9
load factor is **~2,740 GWh/yr**:

| Denominator | Annual load | Campus share | Basis |
|---|--:|--:|---|
| AEP Ohio retail (EIA-861) | ~49,000 GWh | **~5.6%** | `[reference]` transcribed; *verify* |
| PJM total load (EIA-930) | ~800,000 GWh | ~0.34% | `[reference]` transcribed; *verify* |
| Ohio retail (EIA) | ~149,000 GWh | ~1.8% | `[connector]` (shared with [#91](ECONOMICS.md)) |

The headline: **a single campus equals a material fraction (~5–6%) of its serving

verification with a keyed/bulk pull (see the
[reference README](../data/reference/eia/README.md)).

## #95 — interchange layer (EIA-930)

Where do the campus's electrons come from? `bosc.grid.interchange` adds an **EIA-930**
connector (`fetch_ba_interchange`, regenerated by `bosc interchange` →
[`data/reference/eia/ba-interchange.yaml`](../data/reference/eia/)) that pulls the BA's
hourly **demand / net generation / total net interchange** and **reduces it to
window-mean aggregates inside the fetch** (so the cached payload stays tiny). Sign
convention: total interchange `TI` is **+ for net exports, − for net imports**.

For PJM over a representative summer month `[connector: EIA-930]`:

| | Mean (MW) |
|---|--:|
| Demand | ~101,800 (peak ~152,000) |
| Net generation | ~104,500 |
| Net interchange | **+2,700** (net **exporter**); range −6,200 … +10,400 |
| Net-import hours | ~24% |

**The derived comparison.** PJM's in-BA net generation exceeds demand by ~2,700 MW on
average, so the campus's ~348 MW added load is **within the in-BA generation headroom —
comfortably met by in-BA generation without requiring net imports** — though it is
~13% of the mean net-interchange swing (~0.34% of PJM demand). This is a **screening**
view over window means, not an hourly dispatch or locational (LMP/congestion) model;
deliverability to the campus's specific bus is the PJM-queue layer (#96).
