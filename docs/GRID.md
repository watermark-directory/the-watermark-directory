# Grid & regulatory stack — utility → interchange → RTO → FERC → federal policy

The layer **above** the facility power model ([COMPUTE.md](COMPUTE.md) /
`bosc.facility.power`): the chain from the campus load up through the serving electric
utility, balancing-authority interchange, the PJM wholesale market, FERC's federal
jurisdiction, and federal energy policy. It is the macro backdrop the consumer-fuel-cost
thread ([ECONOMICS.md §6](ECONOMICS.md)) and the generation-cycle model ([COMPUTE.md](COMPUTE.md))
plug into. Epic **#93**; this page tracks the stack as its sub-issues land.

Like the other computed subsystems, every figure is provenance-tagged — numeric values
as `ProvenancedValue`, string identifications as `CitedFact` (the non-numeric analogue),
so a serving utility is **cited, never asserted**. External pulls follow the connector
contract (`fn(..., settings) -> pydantic` via `_cache.cached_get`, committed fixtures,
offline fallback) and land as regenerable committed datasets under `data/reference/`.

## #95 — interchange layer (EIA-930)

Where do the campus's electrons come from? `bosc.grid.interchange` adds an **EIA-930**
connector (`fetch_ba_interchange`, regenerated by `bosc interchange` →
[`data/reference/eia/ba-interchange.yaml`](../data/reference/eia/)) that pulls the BA's
hourly **demand / net generation / total net interchange** and **reduces it to
window-mean aggregates inside the fetch** (so the cached payload stays tiny). Sign
convention: total interchange `TI` is **+ for net exports, − for net imports**.

For PJM over a representative summer month `[connector: EIA-930]`:

| | Mean (MW) |
|---|--:|
| Demand | ~102,137 (peak ~147,976) |
| Net generation | ~107,884 |
| Net interchange | **+5,753** (net **exporter**); range −837 … +11,509 |
| Net-import hours | ~0.6% (4 of 720 h) |

**The derived comparison.** PJM's in-BA net generation exceeds demand by ~5,747 MW on
average, so the campus's ~348 MW added load is **within the in-BA generation headroom —
comfortably met by in-BA generation without requiring net imports** — though it is
~6% of the mean net-interchange swing (~0.34% of PJM demand). This is a **screening**
view over window means, not an hourly dispatch or locational (LMP/congestion) model;
deliverability to the campus's specific bus is the PJM-queue layer (#96).

## #96 - RTO / wholesale market (PJM)

`bosc.grid.market.derive_pjm_market_scenario` (regenerated by `bosc pjm` →
[`data/reference/pjm/pjm-market.yaml`](../data/reference/pjm/)) situates the campus load
against the three PJM price signals a large new load actually sees, all transcribed
published figures (no live connector; `[reference]`, flagged *verify*):

- **Zonal LMP** (energy price): AEP-zone average annual ~**$35/MWh** (PJM Data Miner 2;
  LMP = energy + congestion + losses, so it varies by node and hour).
- **Capacity market (RPM)**: the **2025/2026 Base Residual Auction** cleared RTO-wide at
  ~**$269.92/MW-day** — an ~9× spike from the prior year (~$28.92/MW-day).
- **Large-load interconnection queue**: PJM has reported on the order of **tens of GW**
  (~50 GW, order-of-magnitude) of large-load / data-center interconnection interest.

**The scenario** (from the first-class `facility_draw`, ~348 MW, [#87](COMPUTE.md), at a
0.9 load factor → ~2,743 GWh/yr):

| Price signal | Rate | Campus footprint |
|---|--:|--:|
| Energy (zonal LMP) | $35/MWh | ~**$96M/yr** |
| Capacity (RPM clearing) | $269.92/MW-day | ~**$34M/yr** |
| Large-load queue | ~50 GW | campus ~0.7% of queue |

The ~**$34M/yr capacity obligation** a single large new load implies at the post-spike
clearing price is the striking figure. A screening "bracket, don't overclaim" view — not
a settlement/dispatch/RPM-bidding model (LMP varies by node/hour; the clearing price is
not the campus's contracted rate; the queue figure is order-of-magnitude).

## #97 - FERC regulatory seam

`bosc.grid.ferc.derive_ferc_seam` (regenerated by `bosc ferc` →
[`data/reference/ferc/ferc-seam.yaml`](../data/reference/ferc/)) models which government
sets the rules for the campus's power. Every statement is a cited `CitedFact` — the
general law at high confidence, the dockets and Form-1 pointer flagged *verify*.

**The jurisdictional map** is the wholesale/retail line of the Federal Power Act:

- **FERC** (FPA §§205/206) — wholesale sales, interstate transmission, and the RTO/ISO
  (PJM) market rules, including co-location / behind-the-meter at FERC-jurisdictional
  generators.
- **PUCO** (FPA §201(b) reserves retail to the states) — Ohio retail electric service,
  distribution, and retail rates.
- **Campus arrangement** — classified **PUCO retail (grid-served)**: a retail tariff
  customer of AEP Ohio (#94), so its service and rate are PUCO-regulated; a
  behind-the-meter co-location at a generator would instead implicate FERC.

**Captured large-load / co-location dockets** (real FERC proceedings, public records,
verify-flagged): `ER24-2172` (Susquehanna–AWS co-location ISA amendment, FERC **rejected**
2024-11-01), `AD24-11-000` (FERC technical conference on large loads co-located at PJM
generators), and the broader PJM co-location/large-load policy proceeding (described, not
pinned). FERC **Form 1** for Ohio Power Company is captured as a cited pointer to FERC
Online, not an asserted figure.

**Cross-reference to economics:** the campus's grid arrangement determines which regulator
sets its price — PUCO retail (grid-served) vs FERC wholesale (co-location) — the regulatory
backdrop the consumer-cost thread ([ECONOMICS.md §6](ECONOMICS.md), #91) sits under.

## #98 - federal energy policy & output

The top of the stack. `bosc.grid.policy.derive_federal_backdrop` (regenerated by
`bosc federal` → [`data/reference/federal/federal-energy.yaml`](../data/reference/federal/))
reads the campus load against the federal backdrop and sizes it against US figures.

**Policy levers** — the federal clean-energy levers bearing on a load/generation of this
profile, each mapped to its direction on cost (a direction, never a quantified offset):
IRA §45 PTC / §48 ITC and the tech-neutral §45Y/48E successors (**lower clean-gen cost**);
§45V hydrogen and §45X advanced manufacturing (tangential); DOE Loan Programs Office
(lowers clean-gen cost) and DOE grid resilience (deliverability). Applicability depends on
the campus's **undisclosed** generation/procurement choices.

**Federal output / statistics** (EIA national `[connector]`, live 2025; LBNL/DOE data-center
share `[reference]`): US total net generation ~**4,430 TWh/yr** and US avg retail price
~**13.63 ¢/kWh** (rising) are live EIA seriesid pulls; US data-center use ~**176 TWh in 2023
(~4.4% of US load)**, projected ~**6.7–12%** by 2028, stays a report-cited reference.

**The campus vs the national backdrop:** the ~348 MW campus at a 0.9 load factor
(~2,743 GWh/yr) is ~**1.6% of US data-center load** and ~**0.06% of US total net
generation** — the campus sized against the national data-center demand wave, the backdrop
the consumer-cost thread ([#91](ECONOMICS.md)) reads against.
