Pursuant to Ohio Revised Code Section 3745.07 and the procedures set forth in the Fact Sheet for NPDES Permit Renewal, Shawnee II WWTP, 2026 (page 4), I am writing to request a public meeting on the above-referenced draft NPDES permit. I am a resident of Shawnee Township, Allen County, living within the service area of this facility and downstream of its discharge to the Ottawa River at River Mile 32.5.

These comments are offered both as the basis for the public meeting request and as written comments on the draft permit.

## I. Antidegradation Determination Is Unsupported

The 2026 Fact Sheet states on page 1 that "No antidegradation review was necessary." However, this bare conclusion is contradicted by the record:

- The current permit (*LD) contains an explicit antidegradation finding on its authorization page, citing OAC 3745-1-05, Provision (D)(1)(b)(i), determining that a lowering of water quality in the Ottawa River is necessary. The draft renewal (*MD) omits this finding entirely.
- The Fact Sheet itself, on page 14, states that phosphorus is "limited based on provisions of OAC 3745-33-06(C) and the plant expansion antidegradation evaluation," referencing an antidegradation evaluation that was performed for the *LD modification.
- The Ottawa River at this segment is listed as impaired under Section 303(d) of the Clean Water Act (Fact Sheet, Table 10, page 25), with partial attainment at River Miles 34.6 and 31.1 for nutrient/eutrophication, dissolved oxygen, chronic toxicity, and biological indicators. Sources of impairment at RM 31.1 (downstream of the WWTP) explicitly include "Sanitary Sewer Overflows (SSOs), Municipal Point Source Discharges."
- Table 12 (page 27) shows the Ottawa River has a 1Q10 flow of **0.0 cfs** above the discharge points. During critical low-flow conditions, the effluent from the interactive dischargers constitutes the entirety of the stream flow. Under these conditions, any discharge authorized by this permit directly determines instream water quality.

A permit renewal authorizing continued discharge at 3.0 MGD average design flow to a 303(d)-listed impaired stream with zero low-flow dilution requires more than a conclusory statement that no antidegradation review was necessary. The public is entitled to an explanation of the regulatory basis for this conclusion under OAC Chapter 3745-1.

## II. The Facility Cannot Reliably Meet Its Proposed Effluent Limits

The Fact Sheet's own data demonstrates that Shawnee II WWTP is structurally unable to meet the proposed limits for ammonia:

- **Table 8 (page 24):** Projected Effluent Quality (PEQ) maximum values for ammonia are **5.32 mg/L (summer)** and **7.99 mg/L (winter)**, both exceeding the proposed weekly average limits of 3.4 mg/L and 4.5 mg/L, respectively.
- **Table 1 (page 20):** The facility had **6 effluent violations in the first 10 months of 2025 alone** -- 1 E. coli and 5 ammonia. This follows 2 violations in 2024, 5 in 2022, and 3 in 2021.
- **Page 7:** The Fact Sheet states these violations "were not caused by a known process error or upset condition," indicating that the violations are not attributable to isolated incidents but rather reflect the facility's baseline performance.

EPA's ECHO database (Detailed Facility Report for FRS ID 110001162802, data refreshed 04/10/2026) further reports that the facility has a current compliance status of "Violation Identified" with 5 of the last 12 quarters in noncompliance, including Reportable Noncompliance in three consecutive quarters (Q10-Q12, January through September 2025). The E. coli weekly exceedance in Q1 2025 reached 224% of the permitted limit.

Despite this record, the Fact Sheet's parameter assessment (Table 14, page 30) places ammonia in **Group 2** -- "PEQ < 25% of WQS, no limit recommended" -- because the technology-based limits (BADCT) are more stringent than water quality standards. While this may be methodologically correct for purposes of the WLA analysis, it does not address the fundamental question: if the facility cannot reliably meet its BADCT limits, what measures will bring it into compliance during the permit term? The draft permit and Fact Sheet are silent on this question.

The public is entitled to understand why the agency is proposing to renew a permit with identical limits that the facility is actively and persistently failing to meet.

## III. Phosphorus TMDL Compliance Is Deferred

Part II, Item U of the draft permit and page 14 of the Fact Sheet disclose that the Maumee Watershed Nutrient Total Maximum Daily Load (TMDL) Report, approved September 2023, assigned Shawnee II WWTP an individual wasteload allocation of **750 kg of total phosphorus** for the critical season (March through July). However, compliance with this allocation is being deferred to a separate Maumee Watershed Total Phosphorus NPDES General Permit rather than being incorporated into this individual permit.

The individual permit's proposed phosphorus limits (1.0 mg/L weekly average, 0.67 mg/L monthly average) at a 3.0 MGD design flow would authorize seasonal mass loadings that may substantially exceed the 750 kg TMDL allocation. The Fact Sheet's Table 5 (page 21) shows actual median phosphorus loadings of 1.5-3.0 kg/day during recent years, which over a 153-day critical season would yield approximately 230-460 kg -- within the allocation at current flows. However, the permit authorizes loadings at 3.0 MGD design flow, and the weekly loading limit (11.4 kg/day) over the critical season would authorize approximately 1,744 kg -- more than double the TMDL allocation.

The public should be afforded the opportunity to understand the relationship between the individual permit limits and the TMDL allocation, and why the allocation is not being directly implemented as a seasonal mass limit in this permit.

## IV. SECAP Post-Construction Compliance Monitoring Defers Key Determinations

The Schedule of Compliance (Part I, Section C) establishes a SECAP Post-Construction Compliance Monitoring program that defers until September 1, 2029, several critical determinations including:

- The peak secondary treatment capacity of the facility following the Phase II upgrades;
- Whether the SSO discharge at station 2PK00002006 (Shawnee Country Club, 40.7167 N, 84.1591 W) can be physically eliminated; and
- Whether the equalization basin overflow at station 2PK00002602 can be physically eliminated.

The Fact Sheet's Table 4 (page 21) documents that EQ basin overflows at station 602 have occurred on **22 to 38 days per year** over the past five years, discharging **46 to 91 million gallons annually** of partially treated effluent. Table 3 (page 20) shows that SSOs at station 006 peaked at 38 events in 2021 and have declined but continue to occur.

The excess flow threshold for station 602 remains set at 4.2 MGD -- the pre-upgrade peak hydraulic capacity. The Phase II upgrades include a third SBR basin and new pumps, yet the permit retains the pre-upgrade threshold without explanation. The Fact Sheet (page 8) states that "the County has proposed revisions to these improvements to address the remaining overflows by opting to install a third SBR and is no longer proposing to install an additional EQ basin." The implications of omitting the additional EQ basin for overflow frequency and volume are not analyzed.

## V. Status of the 1996 Federal Consent Decree

On or about February 29, 1996, the United States District Court for the Northern District of Ohio entered a Consent Decree in *United States of America and the State of Ohio v. The Board of County Commissioners of Allen County, Ohio*, Civil Action No. 3:96 CV 7134 (Judge David A. Katz). The decree covered both the Shawnee No. 2 and American Bath wastewater treatment plants, alleged violations of Section 301 of the Clean Water Act (33 U.S.C. Section 1311), and included an injunction requiring compliance with the NPDES permits "and any renewals or modifications thereof" (Section 8.3).

Neither the draft permit, the 2026 Fact Sheet, nor the public notice discloses the existence or current status of this consent decree. Under Section 19.1 of the decree, termination requires written certification by the Board to both U.S. EPA and Ohio EPA, 12 consecutive months of NPDES compliance, concurrence from both agencies, and a motion to the Court. I respectfully request that Ohio EPA disclose in the administrative record:

1. Whether the consent decree has been formally terminated;
2. If so, when and by what court order; and
3. If not, how the continued federal court jurisdiction over permit compliance was considered in preparing the draft renewal.

## VI. Interactive Discharge Modeling Merits Public Discussion

The Fact Sheet (pages 10-11) documents that Shawnee II's discharge is modeled interactively with Lima WWTP (RM 37.6), Husky Lima Refinery (RM 37.11), and PCS Nitrogen (RM 36.87) using the CONSWLA model. The four facilities were allocated together for conservative parameters, with ammonia done separately.

The Fact Sheet proposes a permit expiration of **May 31, 2029** to align renewal of these interactive permits for future modeling purposes. While this alignment is constructive, the public has not had the opportunity to review the wasteload allocations assigned to the other three dischargers or to evaluate whether the cumulative loading is consistent with water quality standards -- particularly given that the Ottawa River has zero flow at 1Q10 and the receiving water is impaired for the same parameters being allocated.

The CONSWLA model inputs (Table 12, page 27) show Lima WWTP at 28.62 cfs (18.5 MGD) average flow, Husky Lima Refinery at 8.09 cfs (5.23 MGD) design flow across 7 outfalls, and PCS Nitrogen at 6.14 cfs (3.97 MGD) average flow. Shawnee II at 3.10 cfs (2.0 MGD average) is the smallest contributor by volume, though its permit authorizes loadings based on a 3.0 MGD design flow. The cumulative average discharge is approximately 45.95 cfs against a stream with 0.0 cfs at 1Q10 -- the entire low-flow stream is effluent-dominated. A public meeting would provide an opportunity to discuss whether the WLA methodology adequately protects water quality under these conditions.

## Request

For the foregoing reasons, I respectfully request that the Director hold a public meeting on draft permit 2PK00002*MD prior to final action. The issues raised herein -- including the unsupported antidegradation determination, persistent permit violations for which the facility has no demonstrated path to compliance, deferred TMDL implementation, the undisclosed federal consent decree, and the effluent-dominated nature of the receiving stream -- are of significant public interest and warrant an opportunity for public participation beyond the written comment process.

I further request that the comment period be extended to allow the public adequate time to evaluate the interactive discharge modeling and to obtain clarification on the status of the 1996 consent decree.
