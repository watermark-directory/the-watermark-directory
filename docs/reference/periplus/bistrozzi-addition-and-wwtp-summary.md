## County WWTP Capacity & Pre-treatment

### The three county plants — none has an industrial pretreatment program

| Plant | OEPA Permit | Avg / Peak Design | Pretreatment | Receiving Stream |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **American Bath** | 2PH00007 | **1.5 MGD / 4.0 MGD** | **None** | Pike Run |
| **American II** | 2PH00006 | **1.2 MGD / 3.6 MGD** | **None** | Dug Run |
| **Shawnee II** | 2PK00002 | 2.0 → **3.0 MGD** / 4.2 → **12.6 MGD** (post-Phase 2) | **None** | Ottawa River |

**Combined county capacity post-Phase 2: 5.7 MGD average.** Every OEPA
fact sheet explicitly states the county has no approved pretreatment
program and no industrial users in the collection system.

### Why this matters for BOSC

BOSC's design discharge is **2.5 MGD at 83°F** — industrial
cooling-tower blowdown, not domestic waste. That load **cannot route
to county plants** without either standing up a county-wide
pretreatment program (multi-year regulatory project) or pre-treating
on-site to domestic-equivalent quality.

### The dual-forcemain solution

The $29.83M BOSC PS+FM splits the flow across two forcemains precisely
because of this constraint:

| Forcemain | Size / Length | Destination | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **FM-1** | 10" → 8" / 20,100 LF | American Bath (split-off) → American II | Carries only the **domestic-equivalent portion** — sanitary, not cooling/process. County keeps revenue on this share. |
| **FM-2** | **16"** / 17,900 LF | **City of Lima** (78" gravity sewer @ Woodward & Grand → Lima WWTP) | Carries the **bulk** of BOSC flow. Lima *has* an approved industrial pretreatment program. |

The 16" FM-2 is hydraulically larger than the 10"→8" FM-1, confirming
the majority goes to Lima. The +47% cost escalation ($20.25M →
$29.83M) between the June 2025 and February 2026 RFQs is explained
entirely by this design change from one forcemain to two.

### Additional constraints

- **American Bath** has been under a **1996 federal consent decree**
  (Judge Katz, N.D. Ohio) with a 1.5 MGD interim loading limit for 30
  years. Adding 2.5 MGD of industrial discharge would require
  re-permitting that decree.
- **American II** already absorbed the Gomer residential sewering
  (April 2023), reducing its headroom below the nominal 1.2 MGD
  design.
- **Shawnee II Phase 2** ($21.95M, OWDA-funded) raises that plant to
  3.0 MGD avg / 12.6 MGD peak — but it's 9 miles from BOSC and serves
  Shawnee Twp residential, not the BOSC corridor. It is parallel
  county investment, not BOSC-enabling.
- **None of the three current NPDES permits** mention BOSC, Bistrozzi,
  or any industrial tributary. Future permit modifications will be
  required when the forcemains come online (Q4 2026 – June 2028).
