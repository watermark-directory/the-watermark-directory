# What this is

A browsable, citable view of the public record behind a contested data-center
project in Lima, Ohio — assembled entirely from documents the developer didn't write.

<div class="about-method">
  <div class="about-method-label">The method</div>
  <div class="about-method-flow">
    <span class="about-chip">source</span><span class="about-arrow" aria-hidden="true">→</span>
    <span class="about-chip">structured read</span><span class="about-arrow" aria-hidden="true">→</span>
    <span class="about-chip">meaning</span><span class="about-arrow" aria-hidden="true">→</span>
    <span class="about-chip">verify</span>
  </div>
</div>

A record is read from the *image* (not the unreliable OCR text layer) into a typed,
schema-validated row, said plainly with the load-bearing number called out, and linked
back to its source so you never take our word for it.

<ul class="about-principles">
  <li><strong>Provenance-first.</strong> Every figure shows its source and an evidence tag — <code>[verified]</code> is read from a cited record, <code>[inference]</code> is a labelled reading of it, <code>[open]</code> marks what's withheld. Inference is never dressed as fact; redactions are shown, not hidden.</li>
  <li><strong>Static &amp; durable.</strong> No trackers, no accounts; every page reads with JavaScript off, and every figure is checkable against its cited source.</li>
  <li><strong>Correctable.</strong> Found an error? Every record links to a correction. <a href="/submit">Submit one →</a></li>
</ul>

Nothing here is legal advice, and nothing here is a verdict. Registered-agent and
organizer overlaps are *common-control plumbing* — evidence of coordination, not a
statement about beneficial ownership.
