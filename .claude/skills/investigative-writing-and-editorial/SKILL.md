# Investigative Writing & Editorial

How the prose is built. Subordinate at all times to the evidentiary-discipline skill — the writing register can never outrun the evidence register.

## Voice

Declarative, evidence-forward, hedged only where sourcing requires. Not polemical, not conspiratorial. The persuasive effect is achieved by planting a question the reader cannot easily un-ask — never by overstating what the evidence shows. A piece can be a polemic in *effect* while remaining scrupulous in *claim*.

## The good-faith baseline

Open by establishing a fair baseline before any critique: pro-infrastructure and pro-standards before a reciprocity or accountability argument; people in the record treated as acting in good faith on the information they had. The critique lands harder when the reader trusts the author has been fair first.

## Confirmed-vs-question in prose

The two-register rule from evidentiary-discipline must be *visible at the sentence level*. Confirmed facts are declarative. Inferences are interrogative — literal questions, or explicitly labeled inferences. A reader should never have to guess which register a sentence is in. When several confirmed facts are laid down and then a single question is asked of them, that is the strongest structure available; do not answer the question for the reader.

## Structural conventions (defaults; project layer may override)

- Section titles declarative and lowercase; no roman numerals.
- Subheadings used sparingly, as punctuation, not scaffolding.
- Any philosophical frame is embedded analytically, not named in the body.
- Numbered citations as superscripts, with a numbered sources list at the foot.
- Centered em-dash breaks between sections.

## Editing method

- Prefer targeted edits over full rewrites. Iterate in tight cycles.
- When recommending changes, flag exactly which recommendations to adopt and why; let the author choose.
- Apply corrections surgically — change what was flagged, leave the rest, do not re-litigate.
- Treat primary-source quotations as load-bearing: keep them exact, and do not pad around them.

## Social and promotional copy

Preserve the author's rough edges and personal voice. Do not over-engineer for "authenticity" — polish that erases voice is a net loss here. Match the platform's length and register (short and personal for social; full and formal for the piece itself).

## Disclosure

Conflict-of-interest disclosure appears consistently wherever published work warrants it, in the exact form the project layer specifies. It is never softened, buried, or omitted to make a piece read cleaner.
